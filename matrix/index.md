    <h3>Overview</h3>
    <p>This matrix maps the psychological processes and tactics used in radicalization, cult recruitment, and online extremism. 
    It synthesizes research from multiple established frameworks:</p>
    
    <h4>Academic Foundations:</h4>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>McCauley & Moskalenko's Pyramid of Radicalization</strong> - Individual, group, and mass radicalization pathways</li>
      <li><strong>Lifton's Eight Criteria for Thought Reform</strong> - Systematic manipulation and control techniques</li>
      <li><strong>Online Extremism Research</strong> - How algorithms and social media accelerate radicalization</li>
      <li><strong>Hassan's BITE Model</strong> - Behavior, Information, Thought, and Emotional control</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Key Stages</h3>
    <p>The matrix covers the complete lifecycle from vulnerability to recovery:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Initial vulnerabilities and grievances</li>
      <li>Contact and recruitment tactics</li>
      <li>Indoctrination and thought control methods</li>
      <li>Isolation and behavioral control</li>
      <li>Deep radicalization and mobilization</li>
      <li>Exit pathways and prevention strategies</li>
      <li>Recovery and deradicalization</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Applications</h3>
    <p>This framework is designed for:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Understanding cult recruitment patterns</li>
      <li>Analyzing online extremism (incels, white supremacy, conspiracy movements)</li>
      <li>Identifying early warning signs</li>
      <li>Developing prevention and intervention strategies</li>
      <li>Supporting deradicalization efforts</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Notes</h3>
    <p>This matrix is intended for educational and research purposes to help identify manipulation tactics 
    and support those affected by extremist ideologies. It applies across various contexts including 
    political extremism, cult organizations, and online radicalization communities.</p>
    
    <p><em>Disclaimer: This is based on academic research and psychological frameworks. For intervention 
    cases, please consult with qualified mental health professionals specializing in cult recovery and deradicalization.</em></p>
